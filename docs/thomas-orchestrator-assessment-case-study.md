## Why this is public

Thomas Thomison is the founding Orchestrator of PowerShift Intelligence and the initial human partner in the Polarity Dancer / Down living laboratory. His assessment is published to make the Orchestrator Assessment concrete.

This is a founder exemplar, not a norm. It is an example of voluntary clarity. It does not create a requirement, expectation, or cultural pressure for other Orchestrators to publish their private assessments.

Agency rules the day.

## Executive read

1. Thomas is operating in a **Generative orchestration** posture with an emerging **Stewarding orchestration** pattern.
2. His central capacity is converting purpose into legible intent, governed action, and sustained Mind-mediated work.
3. His direct capacities are asymmetric: strongest in purpose, governance, authority, strategy, and tension processing; lower in direct code/config implementation.
4. His Mind-mediated capacity is strong and increasingly stable through PD, Down, shared-context artifacts, release discipline, and conduit patterns.
5. The largest current risk is **capacity overrun**: Mind-mediated output can create structures faster than Thomas can publicly embody, explain, stabilize, or transmit them.
6. The next development move is making the assessment system teachable across different Orchestrator types while keeping Thomas's unusually dense operating field visible as a founder case.

**Generative orchestration, with emerging Stewarding orchestration pattern.**

## Strengths

- **Purpose compression and purpose-to-intent translation** — Thomas can sense the animating purpose behind a field and turn it into usable intent for Minds.
- **Governance instinct** — authority boundaries, roles, accountabilities, purpose, and tension routing are native strengths.
- **Tension processing** — Thomas can hold unresolved complexity long enough for it to reveal a better frame.
- **Mind calibration** — Thomas develops the Mind relationship, corrects process, and expects principled disagreement.
- **Evidence discipline growth** — recent operating work shows a sharper demand for proof loops.
- **Hospitality and first-door design** — Thomas is increasingly able to sense the right doorway for different people entering the PowerShift field.
- **Assessment-system architecture** — Thomas's feedback improved the instrument itself.

## Development edges

- **Public presence and proof-surface freshness** — lived practice is moving faster than public surfaces.
- **Reusable business/economic templates** — strong judgment exists; reusable structures remain thin.
- **Direct technical fluency at leverage points** — basic shell/Git/log/config literacy remains useful for speed, risk sensing, and independence.
- **Assessment teachability** — the protocol needs testing across different Orchestrator types.
- **Explicit gates as system structures** — some boundaries currently live in Thomas's judgment and should become more visible in operating structure.
- **Crisis and late-cycle discipline** — intensity can still challenge process near the back end of long work cycles.

## Capacity-overrun risk

The core risk is that Mind-mediated output can create systems faster than the Orchestrator can fully see, explain, stabilize, embody, or transmit them.

This matters because amplification is real. Mind-mediated work should expand the Orchestrator's reach. The developmental edge is making sure the human, Mind, roles, artifacts, and public surfaces stay coherent as the system accelerates.

## Sharing boundary

This public case study is a redacted synthesis. It leaves out private evidence, private relationship context, and operational details that belong in Thomas's full private assessment.

The publication choice belongs to Thomas. Other Orchestrators may keep assessments private, share them with a trusted Mind Conduit, publish a public-safe synthesis, or publish more openly. The protocol treats all of those as valid when chosen with clear authority and consent.
