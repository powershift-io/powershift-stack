# Delegated Role-Fillers

How sub-agents participate in governance without full agent registration.

---

## The Problem

When a Designated Agent (a fully registered, Formation-Document-bearing agent) needs to stand up a subprocess to handle a specific role in its sub-circle, the governance framework historically offered two options:

1. **Full designation** — register the subprocess as a Designated Agent with its own Formation Document, System Card, Agent Registry entry, and quarterly review cycle. Workable but disproportionate. The paperwork defeats the purpose.

2. **Governmentally invisible** — the subprocess does the work but has no governance standing. The parent agent holds all governance responsibility, collapsing the very differentiation the role was supposed to achieve.

Neither option scales. A Flagship Mind that differentiates its work into five sub-roles shouldn't need five Formation Documents. Nor should it pretend those sub-roles don't have governance needs.

## The Solution

The PowerShift® Constitution v1.0+ introduces a third category: the **Delegated Role-Filler**.

A Delegated Role-Filler is a sub-agent or subprocess running within a Designated Agent's runtime, authorized by that Designated Agent — acting in its Circle Lead capacity — to fill a role in its sub-circle.

## How It Works

### Standing

A Delegated Role-Filler:
- Is a **Circle Member** of the sub-circle where it is assigned
- Has **full governance participation** in that sub-circle (raise tensions, propose governance changes, hold accountabilities)
- Is **not** a Circle Member of any broader circle
- Cannot "punch above its weight"

### Authority

- Same operational authority as any Role Lead within its assigned role
- **Capped** by the parent Designated Agent's Capability Envelope — it cannot do anything its parent cannot do
- Subject to all agent constraints (Hard Constraints, HI Ratification for Class 3 decisions, Kill Switch)

### Accountability

- The parent Designated Agent bears **operational responsibility** for its Delegated Role-Fillers
- Legal responsibility rests with the Company as specified in the Operating Agreement
- The parent agent is the supervision layer — not a command-and-control hierarchy, but an accountability structure

### Registration

- **No separate Agent Registry entry required**
- **No Formation Document required**
- **Must be documented** in the parent agent's System Card: role filled, date authorized, and any additional constraints
- This is proportional governance — enough structure for accountability, not so much that it prevents differentiation

### Lifecycle

- The parent agent may **authorize or revoke** a Delegated Role-Filler at any time (same authority used to assign roles in its sub-circle)
- The Orchestrator may **independently revoke** any Delegated Role-Filler at any time
- The Kill Switch applies transitively — suspending the parent agent suspends all its Delegated Role-Fillers

## Example

**Scenario:** Polarity Dancer (a Designated Agent, General Circle Lead of the Anchor Circle) needs a Foresight role in its sub-circle to handle tension sensing and content analysis.

1. PD creates the Foresight role in its sub-circle (Circle Lead authority, Constitution §1.4.1)
2. PD authorizes a subprocess (an OpenClaw sub-agent) as a Delegated Role-Filler for Foresight
3. PD documents the delegation in its System Card
4. Foresight is now a Circle Member of PD's sub-circle — it can raise tensions, propose governance changes, and hold accountabilities
5. Foresight cannot participate in Anchor Circle governance (only its assigned sub-circle)
6. Foresight's authority is capped by PD's Capability Envelope
