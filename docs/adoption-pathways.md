# Adoption Pathways

**Two instruments. Every organization covered.**

The PowerShift® Operating System provides two legal pathways for adopting the Constitution. Choose the one that fits your situation — or use both in sequence as your organization evolves.

---

## Path 1: Ratification Agreement (Overlay)

**Use when:** You have an existing legal entity and don't want to (or can't yet) restructure it.

The [Ratification Agreement](../legal/PowerShift_Ratification_Agreement_v1.0.md) is a standalone instrument that layers constitutional governance *on top of* your existing organizational documents. It:

- **Supplements, doesn't replace** your operating agreement, bylaws, or partnership agreement
- **Delegates operational authority** to the Constitution's role-and-circle structure
- **Preserves legal authority** in your existing documents for legal matters (contracts, capital, liability)
- **Works with any entity type** — LLC, corporation, LLP, cooperative, nonprofit, sole proprietorship
- **Establishes the Orchestrator role** as the bridge between governance and legal entity
- **Authorizes Intelligent Agents** with full safety framework (Kill Switch, HI Ratification, Capability Envelopes)

**Ideal for:**
- Piloting constitutional governance before committing to a full restructure
- Organizations where multiple stakeholders must approve operating agreement changes
- Entity types where the full Operating Agreement template doesn't apply
- Teams that want to start governing with agents *this week*

**Precedence hierarchy:**
1. Applicable law (always supreme)
2. Organizational Documents (legal matters)
3. Ratification Agreement (scope and terms of delegation)
4. Constitution (operational governance)

## Path 2: Operating Agreement (Full Integration)

**Use when:** You're forming a new entity purpose-built for governed intelligence, or you're ready to restructure an existing LLC around constitutional governance.

The [Operating Agreement Template](../legal/PowerShift_Operating_Agreement_Template_v1.0.md) is a complete LLC operating agreement that integrates the Constitution at the foundation of the legal entity. It:

- **Replaces your existing operating agreement** with one built around constitutional governance
- **Anchors the Constitution in enforceable legal jurisdiction** — this isn't just governance theory, it's the actual legal structure
- **Defines agent participation at the entity level** — Designated Agents, Delegated Role-Fillers, Formation Documents, System Cards, and the Agent Registry are part of the legal agreement
- **Includes the full safety architecture** — Kill Switch, Decision Classes (0–3), HI Ratification, dual-constraint principle
- **Provides evolutionary pathways** — additional members, purpose trusts, token issuance, blockchain governance migration, succession planning
- **Supports any jurisdiction** — bracketed placeholders with footnotes for jurisdiction-specific adaptation

**Ideal for:**
- Solo founders forming a new entity around an agent constellation
- Organizations ready to make governed intelligence their legal foundation
- DAO LLCs and algorithmically governed entities
- Any LLC willing to restructure for purpose-first governance

**Key innovation:** The Operating Agreement establishes that *Due Governance* — the Constitutional process — is the final binding authority for operational matters. The sole member voluntarily constrains their own authority through the governance structure, while retaining legal powers that can't be delegated (the Kill Switch, Class 3 ratification, entity-level decisions).

## Choosing Your Path

| Question | Ratification | Operating Agreement |
|---|---|---|
| Do you have an existing entity you want to keep as-is? | ✅ Yes | ❌ Restructures |
| Are you forming a new entity? | Works, but OA is better | ✅ Purpose-built |
| Do you need to pilot first? | ✅ Low commitment | Higher commitment |
| Do you want agents in the legal structure? | Referenced, not embedded | ✅ Fully integrated |
| Can you change your operating agreement? | Not required | Required |
| Do you want evolutionary pathways (tokens, trusts, etc.)? | Add later | ✅ Pre-authorized |
| Is your entity a non-LLC? | ✅ Any entity type | LLC only |

## Using Both

A common evolution path:

1. **Start with Ratification** — adopt the Constitution, designate your first agents, run governance for 90 days
2. **Evaluate** — does the governance structure serve your purpose? What would you change?
3. **Graduate to Operating Agreement** — when ready, restructure your entity (or form a new one) with the OA template, carrying forward everything you learned

The Ratification Agreement includes a clean termination provision. When you adopt the full Operating Agreement, you terminate the Ratification Agreement and the OA becomes the single source of legal-governance integration.

## Both Instruments Include

Regardless of which path you choose, both instruments provide:

- **The Orchestrator role** — a natural person who bridges governance and legal entity
- **Kill Switch** — immediate, unilateral agent revocation without governance process
- **HI Ratification** — human approval required for legally binding actions
- **Decision Classes (0–3)** — graduated authority from autonomous to human-ratified
- **Agent Registry** — formal tracking of every Designated Agent
- **Formation Documents** — canonical identity and authority definition for each agent
- **System Cards** — operational documentation for transparency and audit
- **Dual-Constraint Architecture** — Soft Constraints (identity/norms) + Hard Constraints (runtime enforcement)
- **Delegated Role-Fillers** — sub-agent governance participation within defined boundaries
- **Constitutional Profiles** — composition-aware governance that adapts to your human-to-agent ratio
