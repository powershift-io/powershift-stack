# Why We Forked the Holacracy Constitution

**Date:** April 4, 2026  
**Author:** Thomas Thomison, Orchestrator, powershift.io

---

## The Short Version

The Holacracy® Constitution v5.0 was designed for organizations composed entirely of humans. PowerShift® needed a governance framework for organizations where humans and intelligent agents share authority. Rather than continuing to patch the original with amendments, we forked it into a standalone document that integrates agent governance natively.

## The Tension That Started It

We had a well-formed role definition (Foresight) assigned to a sub-agent operating as a subprocess within our Flagship Mind's workspace. Operationally, everything worked. Governmentally, the sub-agent had no standing — it couldn't participate in the governance of the circle it worked in.

Our framework recognized exactly two categories: **Designated Agents** (registered, Formation Document, full governance rights) and **everything else** (governmentally invisible). There was no intermediate category proportional to what a sub-process actually needs.

This is the classic scaling problem of bureaucratic categories: the cost of full designation was disproportionate to the authority a sub-circle role-filler actually requires. Imagine requiring full corporate officer status for every intern.

## Why Not Keep Amending?

We had already adopted five amendments to the Holacracy base text. Adding a sixth (Delegated Role-Fillers) pushed the "base document + amendments" model past its useful complexity threshold:

- **Cognitive load:** The reader must mentally merge two documents to understand the operative framework
- **Interaction effects:** Amendments began referencing each other — Amendment 1's agent definitions intersected with Amendment 5's constraints, which intersected with the new Delegated Role-Filler category
- **Agent readability:** When your governance participants include intelligent agents parsing the Constitution to determine their authority, the "mentally merge two documents" pattern introduces real error risk

## What the License Permits

The Holacracy Constitution v5.0 is published under **CC BY-SA 4.0**. This explicitly permits forking, modification, and commercial use. The requirements:

1. Credit HolacracyOne as the original author ✓
2. Indicate changes were made ✓
3. Distribute under the same CC BY-SA 4.0 license ✓
4. Don't use the "Holacracy" trademark on the derivative ✓

The HolacracyOne GitHub repository explicitly addresses forking: "You may not name or otherwise brand your derivative work using the Holacracy mark, to avoid confusion between your version and the official Holacracy document." We already branded ours as **The PowerShift® Constitution**.

## What Changed in the Fork

### Integrated from Existing Amendments
- Intelligent agents as governance participants (Partner status for Designated Agents)
- The Orchestrator role (bridging governance and legal entity)
- Programmatic governance (authenticated API-based governance participation)
- Agent constraint framework (Capability Envelopes, Hard Constraints, Kill Switch)
- Self-modification prohibition for agents

### New in the Fork
- **Delegated Role-Fillers** (§1.2.7, §4.1.B) — sub-agents with proportional governance standing
- **Natural language rewrite** (v1.1) — same rules, dramatically more accessible
- **Composition-aware profiles** — the framework adapts its weight to the human-to-agent ratio

### What Didn't Change
Every authority rule from the Holacracy base survives. The tension → proposal → objection → integration engine is identical. Circles, roles, domains, policies, accountabilities — all intact. The structural DNA is preserved. The extensions are additive.

## The Relationship to Holacracy

PowerShift® is an evolution of the Holacracy tradition, not a competitor. Thomas Thomison co-founded HolacracyOne and spent over two decades developing and practicing self-management governance. The PowerShift® Constitution carries that DNA forward into a world where organizational intelligence is no longer exclusively human.

We honor the original by preserving its structural logic, maintaining CC BY-SA 4.0 licensing, and crediting HolacracyOne clearly. We extend it by addressing a category of organizational participant that didn't exist when the original was written.

The fork is open. Fork ours.
